What Traceline does

Five things your codebase needs after a bug report.

Each stage stands on its own — jump straight to a patch, or start from a plain-English explanation. Traceline adapts to how deep you want to go.

Understand existing code

Get a map of what a file, module, or service actually does — dependencies, data flow, and side effects included.

Pinpoint the bug

Stop scanning stack traces by hand. Traceline narrows a failure down to the exact function and line.

Explain why it happens

A plain-language root cause, not just an error name — written so anyone on the team can follow it.

Suggest the fix

A working patch with a confidence score, plus alternatives when there's a real tradeoff to weigh.

Generate a patch or PR

Review a real diff, then open it as a pull request with a description already written for your reviewers.

Suggested fixes

See the fix before you commit to it.

Every suggestion arrives as a real diff, not a vague suggestion. Compare it against the original, read the reasoning, and check it against alternatives before anything touches your branch.

  • Side-by-side diffs — see exactly what changes, line by line.

  • Alternative approaches — a minimal patch or a deeper refactor, your call.
